System Architecture

A simple explanation of the heavy-duty math protecting your secrets.

1. What is "Zero-Knowledge"? (The Magic Box)

Normally, when you use a website, you send your password across the internet to their computer, hoping they keep it safe. Zero-Knowledge means your secrets never leave your screen. Think of this tool as a magic steel box: you put your passwords inside, lock it using your 3 words, and the tool immediately "forgets" the words. The tool, the internet, and the person who made it have "zero knowledge" of your secret.

2. How the Lock Works (The Cryptography)

  • PBKDF2 (The 100,000-Step Maze): When you type your 3 words, the computer doesn't just lock the box. It runs your words through a massive mathematical maze exactly 100,000 times! To you, this takes just a second. But if a bad guy's computer tries to guess millions of passwords, they are forced to run that giant maze every single time, slowing them down to a crawl.
  • Salt & IV (The Secret Spice): Hackers use giant "cheat sheets" of locked passwords to crack them instantly. To beat this, the tool throws a completely random handful of digital "spice" (called a Salt) into the math every time you make a vault. Because of this spice, even if you use the exact same 3 words twice, the final locked files will look totally different!
  • AES-GCM (The Military Steel): This is the actual lock on the box. It is the exact same unbreakable math used by the military. The "GCM" part acts like a magical tamper-proof seal. If a hacker tries to mess with even a single letter of your locked file, the seal shatters and the vault completely shuts down, refusing to open.

3. Can a Hacker Guess My Words? (The Beach)

Answer: Practically, NO. It would take thousands of years.

Hackers use "Brute Force," which means having a computer guess every word in the dictionary super fast. But here is why that fails against your vault:

  • The Math of 3 Words: Picking 3 random words out of a dictionary creates over 456 Billion possible combinations. It's like trying to guess exactly which 3 grains of sand you picked from a massive beach.
  • The Speed Limit: Because the bad guy's computer gets trapped in the "100,000-Step Maze" for every single guess, it can't guess fast enough. Checking all 456 billion combinations would take their supercomputers hundreds or thousands of years!

The Only Weakness: YOU. If you pick easy words like "apple banana cat" or "one two three", a hacker will guess the right grains of sand very quickly. Always use unrelated, weird words like "quantum blanket neon" to keep your vault safe!
